Designing agents to design

@CMU

I am a recent Ph.D. graduate from Carnegie Mellon University and am currently a Postdoctoral Associate. I am advised by Jonathan Cagan and Chris McComb. My thesis focuses on developing high-performing agents for complex design challenges in engineering. It proposes several agent frameworks that enable discovery, representation, and generalization of strategies in arbitrarily complex action spaces of creative design problems. My research enables learning from scratch in arbitrarily complex action spaces using deep learning and a tree search-based dual-process framework.

Prior to my Ph.D. I graduated from the Indian Institute of Technology Jodhpur in 2017 with a Bachelor of Technology in Mechanical Engineering. I was advised by Kaushal Desai, Suril V. Shah for my undergraduate thesis.

Apart from academics, I’m interested in Photography, Chess, and Hiking
